Basics of Arabic Grammar

1. Nouns (Ism)

Nouns in Arabic are words that refer to a person, place, thing, or idea. They can be definite or indefinite.

2. Verbs (Fi'l)

Verbs in Arabic indicate actions or states of being. They are conjugated based on tense, person, and number.

3. Particles (Harf)

Particles are words that do not have a complete meaning on their own and are used to connect words and sentences.

4. Sentence Structure

Arabic sentences can be nominal (starting with a noun) or verbal (starting with a verb).
